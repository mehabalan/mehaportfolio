My Story

A Journey Beyond Books

From the soil of a village to the screens of technology — the story of Mehabalan.

Childhood & Early Life

Mehabalan, the youngest son of Sethuraja and Sasikala, was born in Thamaraikulam, a growing village in Ramanathapuram, Tamil Nadu. Loved deeply by his elder brothers, Manobalan and Vinobalan, he grew up in a close-knit farmer family.

Unlike most kids his age, life lessons came outside classrooms. By 5th standard, he was confidently driving cars, bikes, and even heavy transport vehicles — skills far beyond his years. Farming, plumbing, and basic electrical work were everyday learnings, shaping his problem-solving mindset early in life.

The Creative Spark – YouTube in 2018

In 2018, while still in school, Mehabalan explored his creative side by starting a YouTube channel. Shooting, editing, and posting — all done by himself — he documented random moments and vlogs. Though not always consistent, it revealed his independent and creative nature early on.

College Life – The Wanderer

In 2020, he joined RVS College of Engineering, Coimbatore, pursuing Computer Science and Engineering. But he wasn't the typical studious college kid — he wandered through Coimbatore, Tirupur, and Erode, enjoying life more than lectures.

His carefree attitude led to 6 arrears. But in his final semester, he shocked everyone by clearing 11 papers in a single attempt, proving his quick learning ability when focused. This was his first major comeback.

Bangalore – Lost in Translation, Quick in Adaptation

After graduation, Mehabalan moved to Bangalore in search of opportunities. On his first day, he couldn't understand a word of Kannada — even the bus conductor's words felt like an unknown world.

But within 15 days, he learned 80% of Kannada — enough to understand and speak confidently. His Kannada-speaking roommate admired his speed of learning. During this time, he also self-studied Core Java, showing once again his ability to master complex skills on his own.

Job Struggles, Yet Happiness

Like many freshers, Mehabalan faced the classic dilemma: "Everyone wants experience before giving you a job, but how to get experience without a job?"

For six months, he attended countless interviews but faced rejections. Yet, he embraced Bangalore's climate, made friends, and continued vlogging — capturing the beauty of life even without a job.

Chennai – The Breakthrough

After six months in Bangalore, he moved to Chennai for a fresh start. Here, he finally got his break — joining the IT industry as a QA Intern.

From there, he grew into a Software Tester and QA Analyst, mastering Manual and Automation Testing for web and mobile apps, API testing, and advanced QA methodologies using tools like Jira, SonarQube, WinSCP, and PuTTY.

"From the soil of a village to the screens of technology, my journey proves that learning has no boundaries."
